What Is a psi?

A psi is a discrete magical effect, a single shaping of the magical energies that suffuse the multiverse into a specific, limited expression. In casting a psi, a character carefully plucks at the invisible strands of raw magic suffusing the world, pins them in place in a particular pattern, sets them vibrating in a specific way, and then releases them to unleash the desired effect--in most cases, all in the span of seconds.
psis can be versatile tools, weapons, or protective wards. They can deal damage or undo it, impose or remove conditions, drain life energy away, and restore life to the dead.
Uncounted thousands of psis have been created over the course of the multiverse's history, and many of them are long forgotten. Some might yet lie recorded in crumbling psibooks hidden in ancient ruins or trapped in the minds of dead gods. Or they might someday be reinvented by a character who has amassed enough power and wisdom to do so.

psi Level

Every psi has a level from 0 to 9. A psi's level is a general indicator of how powerful it is, with the lowly (but still impressive) magic missile at 1st level and the earth-shaking wish at 9th. Cantrips--simple but powerful psis that characters can cast almost by rote--are level 0. The higher a psi's level, the higher level a psicaster must be to use that psi.
psi level and character level don't correspond directly. Typically, a character has to be at least 17th level, not 9th level, to cast a 9th-level psi.

Known and Prepared psis

Before a psicaster can use a psi, he or she must have the psi firmly fixed in mind, or must have access to the psi in a magic item. Members of a few classes, including bards and psionics, have a limited list of psis they know that are always fixed in mind. The same thing is true of many magic-using monsters. Other psicasters, such as clerics and Technomancers, undergo a process of preparing psis. This process varies for different classes, as detailed in their descriptions.
In every case, the number of psis a caster can have fixed in mind at any given time depends on the character's level.

psi Slots

Regardless of how many psis a caster knows or prepares, he or she can cast only a limited number of psis before resting. Manipulating the fabric of magic and channeling its energy into even a simple psi is physically and mentally taxing, and higher- level psis are even more so. Thus, each psicasting class's description (except that of the Symbiote) includes a table showing how many psi slots of each psi level a character can use at each character level. For example, the 3rd-level Technomancer Umara has four 1st-level psi slots and two 2nd-level slots.
When a character casts a psi, he or she expends a slot of that psi's level or higher, effectively "filling" a slot with the psi. You can think of a psi slot as a groove of a certain size--small for a 1st-level slot, larger for a psi of higher level. A 1st-level psi fits into a slot of any size, but a 9th-level psi fits only in a 9th-level slot. So when Umara casts magic missile, a 1st-level psi, she spends one of her four 1st-level slots and has three remaining.
Finishing a long rest restores any expended psi slots.
Some characters and monsters have special abilities that let them cast psis without using psi slots. For example, a Operative who follows the Way of the Four Elements, a Symbiote who chooses certain eldritch invocations, and a pit fiend from the Nine Hells can all cast psis in such a way.

Casting a psi at a Higher Level

When a psicaster casts a psi using a slot that is of a higher level than the psi, the psi assumes the higher level for that casting. For instance, if Umara casts magic missile using one of her 2nd-level slots, that magic missile is 2nd level. Effectively, the psi expands to fill the slot it is put into.
Some psis, such as magic missile and cure wounds, have more powerful effects when cast at a higher level, as detailed in a psi's description.

Casting in Armor

Because of the mental focus and precise gestures required for psicasting, you must be proficient with the armor you are wearing to cast a psi. You are otherwise too distracted and physically hampered by your armor for psicasting.

Cantrips

A cantrip is a psi that can be cast at will, without using a psi slot and without being prepared in advance. Repeated practice has fixed the psi in the caster's mind and infused the caster with the magic needed to produce the effect over and over. A cantrip's psi level is 0.

Rituals

Certain psis have a special tag: ritual. Such a psi can be cast following the normal rules for psicasting, or the psi can be cast as a ritual. The ritual version of a psi takes 10 minutes longer to cast than normal. It also doesn't expend a psi slot, which means the ritual version of a psi can't be cast at a higher level.
To cast a psi as a ritual, a psicaster must have a feature that grants the ability to do so. The Medic and the Naturalist, for example, have such a feature. The caster must also have the psi prepared or on his or her list of psis known, unless the character's ritual feature specifies otherwise, as the Technomancer's does.
