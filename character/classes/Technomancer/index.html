Class Features

As a Technomancer, you gain the following class features.

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d6 per Technomancer level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 6 + your Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per Technomancer level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: None
Weapons: Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows
Tools: None
Saving Throws: Intelligence, Wisdom
Skills: Choose two from Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, and Religion

Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background: (a) a quarterstaff or (b) a dagger (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack A psibook

psicasting

As a student of arcane magic, you have a psibook containing psis that show the first glimmerings of your true power.

Cantrips

At 1st level, you know three cantrips of your choice from the Technomancer psi list. You learn additional Technomancer cantrips of your choice at higher levels, as shown in the Cantrips Known column of the Technomancer table.

psibook

At 1st level, you have a psibook containing six 1st- level Technomancer psis of your choice. Your psibook is the repository of the Technomancer psis you know, except your cantrips, which are fixed in your mind.

Preparing and Casting psis

The Technomancer table shows how many psi slots you have to cast your psis of 1st level and higher. To cast one of these psis, you must expend a slot of the psi's level or higher. You regain all expended psi slots when you finish a long rest.
You prepare the list of Technomancer psis that are available for you to cast. To do so, choose a number of Technomancer psis from your psibook equal to your Intelligence modifier + your Technomancer level (minimum of one psi). The psis must be of a level for which you have psi slots.
For example, if you're a 3rd-level Technomancer, you have four 1st-level and two 2nd-level psi slots. With an Intelligence of 16, your list of prepared psis can include six psis of 1st or 2nd level, in any combination, chosen from your psibook. If you prepare the 1st-level psi magic missile, you can cast it using a 1st-level or a 2nd-level slot. Casting the psi doesn't remove it from your list of prepared psis.
You can change your list of prepared psis when you finish a long rest. Preparing a new list of Technomancer psis requires time spent studying your psibook and memorizing the incantations and gestures you must make to cast the psi: at least 1 minute per psi level for each psi on your list.

psicasting Ability

Intelligence is your psicasting ability for your Technomancer psis, since you learn your psis through dedicated study and memorization. You use your Intelligence whenever a psi refers to your psicasting ability. In addition, you use your Intelligence modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a Technomancer psi you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

psi save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

psi attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

Ritual Casting

You can cast a Technomancer psi as a ritual if that psi has the ritual tag and you have the psi in your psibook. You don't need to have the psi prepared.

psicasting Focus

You can use an arcane focus as a psicasting focus for your Technomancer psis.

Learning psis of 1st Level and Higher

Each time you gain a Technomancer level, you can add two Technomancer psis of your choice to your psibook for free. Each of these psis must be of a level for which you have psi slots, as shown on the Technomancer table. On your adventures, you might find other psis that you can add to your psibook.

Arcane Recovery

You have learned to regain some of your magical energy by studying your psibook. Once per day when you finish a short rest, you can choose expended psi slots to recover. The psi slots can have a combined level that is equal to or less than half your Technomancer level (rounded up), and none of the slots can be 6th level or higher.
For example, if you're a 4th-level Technomancer, you can recover up to two levels worth of psi slots. You can recover either a 2nd-level psi slot or two 1st-level psi slots.

Arcane Tradition

When you reach 2nd level, you choose an arcane tradition, shaping your practice of magic through one of eight schools: Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, Evocation, Illusion, Necromancy, or Transmutation, all detailed at the end of the class description.
Your choice grants you features at 2nd level and again at 6th, 10th, and 14th level.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

psi Mastery

At 18th level, you have achieved such mastery over certain psis that you can cast them at will. Choose a 1st-level Technomancer psi and a 2nd-level Technomancer psi that are in your psibook. You can cast those psis at their lowest level without expending a psi slot when you have them prepared. If you want to cast either psi at a higher level, you must expend a psi slot as normal.
By spending 8 hours in study, you can exchange one or both of the psis you chose for different psis of the same levels.

Signature psis

When you reach 20th level, you gain mastery over two powerful psis and can cast them with little effort. Choose two 3rd-level Technomancer psis in your psibook as your signature psis. You always have these psis prepared, they don't count against the number of psis you have prepared, and you can cast each of them once at 3rd level without expending a psi slot. When you do so, you can't do so again until you finish a short or long rest.
If you want to cast either psi at a higher level, you must expend a psi slot as normal.

Arcane Traditions

The study of Technomancerry is ancient, stretching back to the earliest mortal discoveries of magic. It is firmly established in fantasy gaming worlds, with various traditions dedicated to its complex study.
The most common arcane traditions in the multiverse revolve around the schools of magic. Technomancers through the ages have cataloged thousands of psis, grouping them into eight categories called schools. In some places, these traditions are literally schools; a Technomancer might study at the School of Illusion while another studies across town at the School of Enchantment. In other institutions, the schools are more like academic departments, with rival faculties competing for students and funding. Even Technomancers who train apprentices in the solitude of their own towers use the division of magic into schools as a learning device, since the psis of each school require mastery of different techniques.

School of Evocation

You focus your study on magic that creates powerful elemental effects such as bitter cold, searing flame, rolling thunder, crackling lightning, and burning acid. Some evokers find employment in military forces, serving as artillery to blast enemy armies from afar. Others use their spectacular power to protect the weak, while some seek their own gain as bandits, adventurers, or aspiring tyrants.

Evocation Savant

Beginning when you select this school at 2nd level, the gold and time you must spend to copy an evocation psi into your psibook is halved.

Sculpt psis

Beginning at 2nd level, you can create pockets of relative safety within the effects of your evocation psis. When you cast an evocation psi that affects other creatures that you can see, you can choose a number of them equal to 1 + the psi's level. The chosen creatures automatically succeed on their saving throws against the psi, and they take no damage if they would normally take half damage on a successful save.

Potent Cantrip

Starting at 6th level, your damaging cantrips affect even creatures that avoid the brunt of the effect. When a creature succeeds on a saving throw against your cantrip, the creature takes half the cantrip's damage (if any) but suffers no additional effect from the cantrip.

Empowered Evocation

Beginning at 10th level, you can add your Intelligence modifier to one damage roll of any Technomancer evocation psi you cast.

Overchannel

Starting at 14th level, you can increase the power of your simpler psis. When you cast a Technomancer psi of 1st through 5th level that deals damage, you can deal maximum damage with that psi.
The first time you do so, you suffer no adverse effect. If you use this feature again before you finish a long rest, you take 2d12 necrotic damage for each level of the psi, immediately after you cast it. Each time you use this feature again before finishing a long rest, the necrotic damage per psi level increases by 1d12. This damage ignores resistance and immunity.

Your psibook

The psis that you add to your psibook as you gain levels reflect the arcane research you conduct on your own, as well as intellectual breakthroughs you have had about the nature of the multiverse. You might find other psis during your adventures. You could discover a psi recorded on a scroll in an evil Technomancer's chest, for example, or in a dusty tome in an ancient library.
Copying a psi into the Book. When you find a Technomancer psi of 1st level or higher, you can add it to your psibook if it is of a psi level you can prepare and if you can spare the time to decipher and copy it. Copying that psi into your psibook involves reproducing the basic form of the psi, then deciphering the unique system of notation used by the Technomancer who wrote it. You must practice the psi until you understand the sounds or gestures required, then transcribe it into your psibook using your own notation.
For each level of the psi, the process takes 2 hours and costs 50 gp. The cost represents material components you expend as you experiment with the psi to master it, as well as the fine inks you need to record it. Once you have spent this time and money, you can prepare the psi just like your other psis.
Replacing the Book. You can copy a psi from your own psibook into another book - for example, if you want to make a backup copy of your psibook. This is just like copying a new psi into your psibook, but faster and easier, since you understand your own notation and already know how to cast the psi. You need spend only 1 hour and 10 gp for each level of the copied psi.
If you lose your psibook, you can use the same procedure to transcribe the psis that you have prepared into a new psibook. Filling out the remainder of your psibook requires you to find new psis to do so, as normal. For this reason, many Technomancers keep backup psibooks in a safe place. The Book's Appearance. Your psibook is a unique compilation of psis, with its own decorative flourishes and margin notes. It might be a plain, functional leather volume that you received as a gift from your master, a finely bound gilt-edged tome you found in an ancient library, or even a loose collection of notes scrounged together after you lost your previous psibook in a mishap.
